# Summary

Genome-wide association studies have identified over 75 Alzheimer's disease (AD) risk loci enriched in myeloid cell pathways, but how this genetic risk distributes across organs and cell types in spatial context is unknown. Here, we projected AD GWAS signals onto whole-body spatial transcriptomics data spanning 15 mouse organs using gsMap, and extended the analysis across 10 age points (1–30 months) by integrating eight single-cell RNA-seq datasets. We found that AD genetic risk is carried by tissue-resident macrophages in every organ, but colonic epithelial cells harbor an independent enrichment driven by APP processing genes (APP, SORL1, PSEN1, ADAM10, CD2AP). This gut epithelial program was AD-specific: absent in Parkinson's disease, which instead showed oligodendrocyte-driven brain enrichment. The spatial risk pattern was stable across the mouse lifespan. These findings reveal two distinct peripheral pathways of AD genetic risk — myeloid and gut epithelial — and provide host genetic evidence for gut-brain axis involvement in AD.
